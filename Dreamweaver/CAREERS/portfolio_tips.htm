How is an electronic portfolio distributed?

What is a portfolio?

A portfolio is a collection of work that clearly illustrates effort, progress, and achievement of knowledge and skills.

What form does it take?

Traditional portfolios typically take the form of a file folder or a three-ring binder. An electronic portfolio stores and presents portfolio content in a digital format such as a website.

 

Pages in both a traditional portfolio and an electronic portfolio website vary depending on the individual and the purpose of creating the portfolio. Examples of pages or heading that might be included on a portfolio designed for a job search are:

Career Objective

Education

Extra-curricular Activities

Work Experience

Volunteer Work

Hobbies

Skills

Awards/Achievements

References

How is an electronic portfolio distributed?

Electronic portfolios can be burned to a CD to distribute with a paper résumé, which gives the potential employer a nice package of both printed and electronic information.
